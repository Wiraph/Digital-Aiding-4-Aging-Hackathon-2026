# Product

## Register

product

## Users
The main users are older adults performing a guided arm-reaching assessment at home. Doctors and physical therapists are secondary users who review left/right arm usage, tracking quality, movement quality, and Learned Non-Use risk. Older adults may be under clinical supervision, may have limited stamina or motor control, and need bright, calm, high-contrast, easy-to-read instructions with large touch targets.

## Product Purpose
Digital Aiding 4 Aging Hackathon 2026 is a camera-based rehabilitation screening tool for a nine-zone arm-reaching task. It helps capture patient profile data, calibrate camera visibility, guide the assessment, summarize scores, and provide clinician-facing evidence for follow-up. It is a screening and monitoring aid, not a medical diagnosis system.

## Brand Personality
Refined, trustworthy, calm. The product should feel like a bright high-end digital health concierge for older adults: precise, respectful, tactile, reassuring, and quietly premium without compromising task clarity.

## Anti-references
Avoid flat generic blue SaaS dashboards, toy-like rehabilitation visuals, noisy gamification, harsh clinical utility screens, and decorative motion that delays the task. Avoid anything that makes clinical controls feel unfamiliar or difficult to operate.

## Design Principles
1. Make the camera and assessment state instantly legible.
2. Treat clinical data as crafted evidence, not decoration.
3. Keep standard controls predictable while making them feel premium.
4. Let motion confirm state changes instead of becoming choreography.
5. Support users with calm contrast, readable Thai text, and reduced-motion alternatives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Aim for WCAG AA contrast for all text and controls. Preserve keyboard focus states, large touch targets, clear labels, and reduced-motion alternatives. Avoid relying on color alone for status, progress, readiness, or score interpretation.
